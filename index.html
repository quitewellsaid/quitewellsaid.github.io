Sample Excerpt

This is what it sounds like

Every eulogy we write is unique — built entirely from the stories you share. Here's an anonymized excerpt.

He was the kind of man who fixed things. Not just the screen door or the leaky faucet — though Lord knows he fixed those too, usually on a Saturday morning with a cup of coffee balanced on the porch rail and a transistor radio playing the Reds game.

He fixed what was broken in people. When Tom lost his job at the mill in '82, it was Dad who showed up with a truck, a cooler of beer, and a plan. When Aunt Ruth's marriage fell apart, it was Dad she called at two in the morning — and he drove forty minutes in the rain without a single complaint.

He graduated from Central High the same spring Kennedy was inaugurated, and he carried that sense of duty with him his whole life. Not the loud kind. The quiet kind — the kind that shows up.

— From a eulogy for a father, delivered by his eldest daughter
